My Personal Interests

I became interested in cyber security at young age, partly due to my unwillingness to concede to
the restrictions my parents would put on my devices, and partly because the idea that nothing is
unhackable fascinated me. I loved the thrill of macspoofing, rendering router restrictions useless,
extracting/bruteforcing restriction passcode hashes, and proving to my friends that I could "guess"
their wifi password. Such experiences have inspired me to want to learn how to identify
vulnerabilities and keep software secure as more and more of our lives involve the internet.

Last summer, my now wife, got me into rock climbing, and it's something I really enjoy doing on
weekends. There's a big difference between outdoor and indoor climbing. I love indoor climbing
when I want to really work on my technique, but outdoor climbing is my favorite as it brings
a certain thrill that you don't get in the climbing gym. I think it has to do with the fact
that if I slip out doors, I'm falling the distance from the last bolt, plus the slack in the
rope. Let's just say that's not a fun slam against the ragged stone.

I served as missionary for my church, and was fortunate enough to learn Spanish during my service
Speaking Spanish has given me the opportunity to connect with many different cultures on a very
personal level. I love hearing the personal experiences of other's who come from very different
countries and backgrounds. I also love listening to Spanish music, such as reggeaton and bachata.
